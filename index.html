I'm Santhakumar Ramesh, a data scientist pursuing my Master's in Data Science and Applications at University at Buffalo. My expertise lies in developing predictive models and creating interactive dashboards that drive data-informed decision-making across healthcare and business domains.

With hands-on experience processing 200,000+ data points and building 87%+ accurate machine learning models, I specialize in end-to-end data pipelines—from raw data wrangling to production-ready analytics. My current research at UB's School of Public Health investigates occupational heavy metal exposure using NHANES datasets, contributing to evidence-based public health policy.

I excel at making sophisticated algorithms accessible to non-technical stakeholders. Whether it's survey-weighted regression for epidemiological research, XGBoost for predictive analytics, or interactive Tableau dashboards for business intelligence, I design solutions that balance statistical rigor with practical usability.

My technical toolkit spans Python, R, SQL, Tableau, and Power BI, complemented by expertise in scikit-learn, XGBoost, and statistical modeling libraries. I'm particularly drawn to projects where analytics can create measurable impact—improving patient outcomes, optimizing operations, or uncovering hidden insights in large datasets.

Beyond coding and modeling, I'm constantly exploring emerging AI technologies and contributing to open-source projects. I believe great data science combines technical excellence with clear communication, turning insights into action.
