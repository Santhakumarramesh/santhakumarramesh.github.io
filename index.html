98.4% Accuracy
0.988 ROC-AUC

  • Feature Engineering: Combined text preprocessing (lemmatization, stopword removal) with hand-crafted signals (text length, caps ratio, has_company_logo)

Motor Balance Analysis | Welding Exposure vs Hoehn & Yahr Stages

University at Buffalo | 2025 | Pilot Research Study

Developed a data-driven framework comparing motor balance impairment between Parkinson's Disease patients and occupational welders, introducing a novel Preliminary Welding Exposure Staging System (W1–W5) modeled after the Hoehn & Yahr neurological scale.

96.7% Fall Risk Accuracy
30 Patients
48 Features
  • Novel Staging Framework: Designed 5-stage welding exposure classification (W1–W5) using clinically readable cutpoints with Cumulative Exposure Index capturing noise, vibration, fume levels, work hours, and PPE usage
  • End-to-End ML Pipeline: Built full ETL + feature engineering pipeline producing 48 features — normalized scores, deficit measures, composite balance indices, and age × balance interaction terms from raw clinical data
  • Cross-Population Prediction: Trained 4 models on PD patients (H&Y ground truth) using only common clinical features, then applied to welders generating H&Y-like severity scores with 71.4% ±1-stage accuracy via LOOCV
  • Multi-Task Classification: Delivered 3 parallel systems: Motor Balance Severity (5-class, RF 96.7%), Group Classification PD vs Welder (LR 93.3%), Fall Risk Prediction (XGBoost 96.7%)
  • Clinical Insights: Spearman correlations revealed H&Y Stage predicts BBS (ρ=−0.553, p<0.05) and FES (ρ=+0.564, p<0.05); 79% PD patients vs 19% welders met high fall-risk threshold
